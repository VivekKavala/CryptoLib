Understanding Bitcoin ETFs Bitcoin exchange-traded funds (ETFs) allow investors to gain exposure to the price of bitcoin through traditional brokerage accounts without needing to buy, store or secure the bitcoin themselves. ETFs have long been a popular financial product, offering investors a way to track assets or baskets of assets, such as stocks, bonds or commodities. As bitcoin has become a more established asset, bitcoin ETFs have emerged as a means for investors to access this digital asset without directly holding or transferring bitcoin. Fidelity’s FBTC spot bitcoin ETF is a prime example of this innovation. What Is A Spot Bitcoin ETF? A spot bitcoin ETF is an investment vehicle that tracks the price of bitcoin by holding actual bitcoin in reserve. Unlike futures-based bitcoin ETFs, which derive their value from contracts predicting the future price of bitcoin, spot bitcoin ETFs aim to reflect the real-time price of bitcoin. Investors in a spot ETF gain exposure to bitcoin’s market performance, but without the complexities of wallet management, private key security or direct custody. How a Spot Bitcoin ETF Works A spot bitcoin ETF works by purchasing and holding bitcoin on behalf of its investors. In Fidelity’s case, the bitcoin is held by Fidelity Digital Asset Services, LLC (FDAS), which serves as the custodian. The fund’s value is tied to the Fidelity Bitcoin Reference Rate, a composite index that tracks bitcoin prices across various exchanges using a volume-weighted median price (VWMP) calculation. This ensures that the ETF’s price closely follows the actual market value of bitcoin. For investors, buying shares of FBTC is as simple as purchasing shares of any other ETF, allowing for convenient access to bitcoin’s performance. Differences Between Spot Bitcoin ETFs And Futures Bitcoin ETFs Spot bitcoin ETFs and futures bitcoin ETFs differ primarily in how they gain exposure to bitcoin. While spot ETFs hold actual bitcoin, futures ETFs rely on contracts that speculate on the future price of bitcoin. This can lead to discrepancies between the ETF's value and the current market price of bitcoin. Spot ETFs like FBTC are generally seen as more straightforward for investors who want their investment to track bitcoin’s spot price closely, whereas futures ETFs are subject to contango, backwardation and other market phenomena that can affect their performance relative to spot prices. How Spot Bitcoin ETFs Track Prices Spot bitcoin ETFs track bitcoin prices by directly holding bitcoin and valuing it according to a market-based index. Fidelity’s FBTC ETF uses the Fidelity Bitcoin Reference Rate, which aggregates bitcoin price data from multiple exchanges. This data is processed using a VWMP method, ensuring that the ETF reflects real-time bitcoin prices with accuracy. By utilizing this index, FBTC provides transparent and reliable tracking of bitcoin’s price in U.S. dollars, offering investors a consistent and clear view of their exposure. Benefits Of Spot Bitcoin ETFs For Investors Spot bitcoin ETFs like Fidelity’s FBTC offer several benefits for investors. First, they provide a regulated and convenient way to invest in bitcoin without the need to navigate the complexities of wallets, private keys or cold storage. Additionally, since the ETF holds actual bitcoin, it tracks the asset’s price more closely than a futures-based ETF. For investors with traditional brokerage accounts, FBTC offers an easy way to diversify into bitcoin while benefiting from regulatory oversight and the trusted infrastructure of Fidelity Investments. What Is Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF? Fidelity’s FBTC spot bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded product designed to give investors exposure to bitcoin’s price movements. The ETF holds bitcoin directly and mirrors the value of bitcoin in real-time through its tracking of the Fidelity Bitcoin Reference Rate. It is listed on the Cboe BZX Exchange under the ticker symbol “FBTC.” Fidelity’s reputation as a leading asset manager provides additional confidence for investors looking to enter the bitcoin market through a regulated and well-established financial institution. Details about FBTC, like most other publicly traded funds, are available in its prospectus. Overview Of Fidelity Investments Fidelity Investments is one of the largest financial institutions in the world, with over $4.5 trillion in assets under management. The company is known for its commitment to innovation, providing a wide range of investment products, including mutual funds, ETFs and retirement accounts. Fidelity’s expansion into the bitcoin space reflects its longstanding belief that bitcoin is a digital asset set apart from all others. Fidelity Digital Assets, the arm responsible for the FBTC ETF, provides custody and trade execution services for institutional and individual investors interested in bitcoin and other digital assets. Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF Overview The FBTC spot bitcoin ETF seeks to track bitcoin’s price performance as measured by the Fidelity Bitcoin Reference Rate. Unlike derivatives-based products, FBTC is a fully collateralized spot ETF, meaning it holds actual bitcoin in reserve. This makes it a simple and effective option for investors looking to track bitcoin’s value without managing the underlying asset themselves. A key difference between FBTC and other spot bitcoin ETFs is that the bitcoin backing FBTC is held in custody by Fidelity. Most other spot bitcoin ETF providers outsource their custody to Coinbase. Depending on your perspective on custody risk, it may be preferable to know that Fidelity itself holds the bitcoin backing FBTC, rather than the responsibility for the integrity of the fund being diffused across several institutions. Key Features Of The FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF FBTC stands out for its low expense ratio of 0.25%, which is competitive compared to other bitcoin investment vehicles. The ETF’s holdings are fully insured and stored in a mix of cold and hot storage, ensuring both liquidity and security. Additionally, FBTC offers daily liquidity, meaning investors can buy and sell shares as needed, with full transparency regarding the fund’s underlying assets. Benefits of Investing in Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF For investors looking to gain exposure to bitcoin in a regulated environment, Fidelity’s FBTC spot bitcoin ETF provides several advantages. Simplified Access To Bitcoin One of the biggest barriers to direct bitcoin investment is the technical complexity involved in purchasing, storing and securing bitcoin. FBTC eliminates these hurdles, allowing investors to buy bitcoin exposure through a familiar brokerage account, just as they would buy any other stock or ETF. Lower Risk Compared To Directly Investing In Bitcoin By holding bitcoin through an ETF, investors trade some of the risks associated with direct bitcoin ownership for others. For example, they trade the risk of being personally responsible for a security breach or losing access to a wallet. On the other hand, they take on counterparty risk, which is the risk that the custodian of the bitcoin that backs the ETF will mismanage or lose access to it. Regulatory Oversight And Security FBTC is subject to the same regulatory scrutiny as other financial products, which provides a level of investor protection not available with direct bitcoin ownership. Additionally, Fidelity Digital Asset Services employs robust security measures, including cold storage for the majority of its bitcoin holdings, ensuring the safety of the underlying assets. Diversification In Traditional Investment Accounts Another benefit of FBTC is that it can be easily incorporated into retirement accounts or other traditional investment portfolios. This allows investors to diversify into bitcoin without disrupting their existing financial strategies. Gain Price Exposure To Bitcoin With Tax Advantaged Accounts One of the key advantages of investing in a spot bitcoin ETF is the ability to gain exposure to the price of bitcoin within tax-advantaged accounts, such as individual retirement accounts (IRAs). Disbursing money from a tax-advantaged retirement account to invest in bitcoin would trigger a tax penalty, but simply converting some of the fund’s contents to FBTC provides bitcoin price exposure without the penalty. (If you prefer to hold physical bitcoin in a tax-advantaged account, rather than a spot bitcoin ETF, there are options for that as well.) Risks To Consider Like all investments, Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF carries certain risks that investors should understand. Counterparty Risk A critical risk to consider when investing in a bitcoin ETF, rather than simply holding physical bitcoin itself, is counterparty risk. This refers to the possibility that a party involved in the ETF's operation could default on their obligations. For a bitcoin ETF, a custodian must be responsible for securely storing the bitcoin held by the fund. If the custodian were to experience a security breach or other event that results in the loss of access to the bitcoin in its custody, the fund would fail. While ETFs are subject to regulatory oversight, ensuring these counterparties follow strict protocols, the risk of mismanagement or technical failures still exists. With any asset, there must ultimately be a custodian. If it’s you, you take on risk of loss or mismanagement yourself; if you outsource this responsibility to a custodian, you also take on counterparty risk. Volatility Of Bitcoin Prices Bitcoin’s price is notoriously volatile, and investing in an ETF that tracks its price can expose investors to sharp fluctuations in value. While FBTC offers an easier way to access bitcoin, it does not shield investors from the asset’s inherent volatility. Regulatory Risks As with all cryptocurrency-related products, there is a degree of regulatory uncertainty. Changes in regulations affecting bitcoin or cryptocurrency markets could impact the value of the FBTC ETF. Liquidity And Market Risks Although FBTC is designed to offer daily liquidity, rapid price movements in the bitcoin market could affect the ETF’s ability to trade at its intended value. This could lead to instances where investors buy or sell shares at a premium or discount relative to the ETF’s net asset value (NAV). Potential Management Fees And Costs While FBTC has a competitive fee structure, investors should still be aware of the costs involved, including management fees and potential trading commissions when buying or selling shares. How to Buy Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF Investing in Fidelity’s FBTC Spot Bitcoin ETF is straightforward and can be done through most brokerage accounts. Eligibility And Requirements To invest in FBTC, you need an account with a brokerage that offers access to the Cboe BZX Exchange, where the ETF is listed. Additionally, you must meet any account minimums set by your broker and understand any associated fees. Open A Brokerage Account With Fidelity If you don’t already have an account, you can open a brokerage account with Fidelity or another broker that provides access to the FBTC ETF. The process involves completing an application, funding your account, and agreeing to the brokerage’s terms and conditions. Buy Shares Of FBTC Once your brokerage account is set up, you can place an order to buy shares of FBTC just as you would with any other ETF or stock. Be mindful of the market price and any associated trading fees. Monitor Investment After purchasing shares of FBTC, it’s essential to regularly monitor your investment. Bitcoin’s price volatility means the value of the ETF can fluctuate significantly, so staying informed about market conditions and any relevant news is crucial. FBTC vs. Other Bitcoin Investments For many people, buying and holding bitcoin directly is the best choice. If you are comfortable with digital devices and apps, and want to benefit from bitcoin’s value proposition as an asset and monetary network independent of the legacy financial system, there are plenty of options for exchanging cash for bitcoin and holding it in self custody. People who prefer the familiarity of legacy financial products may find that FBTC offers a more convenient way to gain exposure to the price of bitcoin. FBTC eliminates the need for wallet management and offers a degree of security and oversight. While other bitcoin ETFs, like those based on futures contracts, have their place, FBTC’s spot structure makes it an attractive choice for investors looking for direct exposure to bitcoin’s price performance. Additionally, crypto-focused stocks offer indirect exposure but often include broader company risks, making FBTC a more focused option. FBTC is unique compared to most other spot bitcoin ETFs in that the manager of the fund, Fidelity, is also the custodian of the underlying physical asset. Bottom Line Fidelity’s FBTC spot bitcoin ETF offers a straightforward, regulated, and secure way to invest in bitcoin without directly owning the asset. For investors seeking exposure to bitcoin within a traditional brokerage account, FBTC provides a convenient entry point with lower risks than directly holding the asset.
