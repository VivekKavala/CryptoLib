Bitcoin Cash

BCH

   Bitcoin Cash, officially BCH, is a result of the hard fork of Bitcoin in the summer of 2017. The arguments behind the split relate to disputes within the Bitcoin community as to how to expand the capacity of the network in processing more transactions. It was designed to increase the block size limit from 1MB to 8MB, then up to 32MB, to allow a higher number of transactions per block. The basic objective was to make transactions cheaper and therefore faster, thus suitable for every day use or peer-to-peer digital currency.
   Since its launch, Bitcoin Cash has garnered much support and adoption from people believing in the vision of a scalable low-cost digital payment system. However, such bigger challenges include volatile price rides related to the market and competition not only from Bitcoin but from other cryptocurrencies that come with scaling solutions. Over time, Bitcoin Cash has emerged as one of the major cryptocurrencies by market capitalization while debates over its effectiveness and future continue to pervade the crypto community.

History of Bitcoin Cash

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) was created in August 2017 through a hard fork from Bitcoin (BTC) due to disagreements within the Bitcoin community over how to scale the network. The primary issue was the block size limit in Bitcoin, which led to slower transaction times and higher fees as demand increased. A faction of the community advocated for increasing the block size to accommodate more transactions per block, resulting in the creation of Bitcoin Cash, with an initial block size of 8MB (later increased to 32MB), as opposed to Bitcoin’s 1MB.

The fork marked a significant moment in cryptocurrency history, with Bitcoin Cash positioning itself as a peer-to-peer electronic cash system that focuses on fast, low-fee transactions. Since its launch, Bitcoin Cash has maintained its identity as a more scalable version of Bitcoin, focusing on everyday payments and transactions. Over the years, it has gone through additional forks and developments, but it remains a prominent player in the cryptocurrency space.

Key Features of Bitcoin Cash

  • Increased Block Size:

    Bitcoin Cash increased the block size limit to 8MB (later 32MB), allowing more transactions to be processed per block compared to Bitcoin.

  • Low Transaction Fees:

    By enabling larger blocks, Bitcoin Cash reduces congestion on the network, resulting in lower transaction fees, making it ideal for small, everyday transactions.

  • Faster Transaction Times:

    The increased block size allows more transactions to be processed faster, which is key for its use as a medium of exchange.

  • On-Chain Scaling:

    Bitcoin Cash focuses on on-chain scaling by increasing block size rather than using second-layer solutions like Bitcoin’s Lightning Network.

Advantages of Bitcoin Cash

  • Scalability:

    The increased block size of Bitcoin Cash allows for more transactions to be processed in a single block, improving scalability and reducing network congestion.

  • Low Transaction Fees:

    Bitcoin Cash transactions typically have lower fees compared to Bitcoin, making it a better option for small, everyday transactions.

  • Wider Adoption for Payments:

    Bitcoin Cash is more focused on being used as a currency for everyday transactions, and as a result, it has seen broader adoption in payments and commerce.

Disadvantages of Bitcoin Cash

  • Less Security Than Bitcoin:

    Because Bitcoin Cash has fewer miners than Bitcoin, it has a lower hash rate, which makes it more vulnerable to potential attacks.

  • Brand Confusion:

    Bitcoin Cash often suffers from brand confusion with Bitcoin, and some users struggle to understand the difference between the two currencies.

  • Volatility and Market Position:

    Bitcoin Cash has seen significant price volatility, and while it remains a top cryptocurrency, its position has fluctuated over time due to competition from other projects.

  • Lack of Second-Layer Solutions:

    Unlike Bitcoin, which is developing second-layer solutions like the Lightning Network for scalability, Bitcoin Cash focuses solely on on-chain scaling, which may limit its long-term flexibility.
